The Yu lab at USC focuses on decoding inter-cellular immune recognitions and intra-cellular molecular programs to enhance the efficacy of immunotherapies.

We leverage single-cell genomics, synthetic biology, and systems immunology to study fundamental principles of how immune cells perceive and integrate antigen information and enviromental cues to mount optimal and effective immune responses.

We ask

(1) How do immune cells “see” foreign antigens to evoke immune responses?
(2) How do immune cells “talk” to other cells to control their cell fate decision?
(3) How can we “rewrite” molecular circuits to alter immune cellular behavior?
